PLANT LIFE

The Amazon rainforest hosts a wide range of ecosystems, including rainforests, flooded forests, and savannas, each with unique plant species.

  • Flooded forests

    Seasonally inundated forests teeming with aquatic and terrestrial wildlife, offering unique biodiversity and complex ecosystems.

  • Canopy Trees

    Seasonally inundated forests teeming with aquatic and terrestrial wildlife, offering unique biodiversity and complex ecosystems.

  • Epiphytes

    Air plants, such as bromeliads and orchids, grow on other plants without harming them, drawing nutrients from the air and rain.
